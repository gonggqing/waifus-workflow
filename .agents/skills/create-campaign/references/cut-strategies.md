# Cut 转场策略 / Cut Transition Strategies

Multi-cut AI video gains impact from the **relationship between cuts** — not just what each cut shows, but how the transition creates meaning. This catalog defines reusable transition strategies that map to character archetypes and campaign goals.

---

## Strategy Catalog

### 高反差 / High Contrast

**What it is:** A dramatic tonal, visual, or emotional shift between adjacent cuts. The two cuts feel like they belong to different worlds — but they're the same character.

**When to use:**
- Characters with 表里不一 (appearance ≠ reality): sweet exterior / lethal interior
- Hidden power characters: lazy receptionist → ancient mage
- Dual-life characters: daytime student → nighttime vigilante
- Characters whose story involves a major transformation

**Design pattern (2-cut PV):**
```
Cut 1: [soft/warm/gentle state] — show the character in their public persona
       Lighting: warm, soft, natural
       Action: small everyday gesture (reading, pouring tea, adjusting glasses)
       Mood: calm, approachable, perhaps slightly boring

Cut 2: [intense/cold/powerful state] — reveal the hidden side
       Lighting: dramatic shift (warm → cold blue, or soft → hard rim light)
       Action: combat stance, supernatural ability, commanding presence
       Mood: dangerous, awe-inspiring, alien to Cut 1
```

**Examples:**
- 艾莉亚 (guild receptionist / hidden archmage): Cut 1 yawning at reception desk, warm afternoon light → Cut 2 void barrier spell, eyes glowing, cold blue-purple energy
- 修仙角色 (gentle healer / battle mode): Cut 1 tending spiritual herbs in morning mist → Cut 2 sword rain descending, battle aura ignited, golden-black contrast

**Intensity levels:**
- **Subtle:** Same setting, different expression (smiling → dead-serious gaze)
- **Moderate:** Same setting, different activity (reading newspaper → magical combat in the same room)
- **Extreme:** Completely different setting + lighting + energy (sunlit garden → thunderstorm battlefield)

### 反讽 / Irony

**What it is:** The second cut recontextualizes or subverts what the viewer assumed from the first. It's not just *different* — it makes you re-evaluate Cut 1.

**When to use:**
- Characters whose public image is a deliberate facade
- Characters with a tragic/comedic gap between aspiration and reality
- Campaign hooks that want to provoke "wait, what?" reactions
- Characters with secrets that reframe their behavior

**Design pattern (2-cut PV):**
```
Cut 1: [establish expectation] — present a clear narrative the viewer accepts
       The character seems to be one thing. The framing supports this reading.

Cut 2: [subvert expectation] — reveal something that makes Cut 1 mean something else
       Same character, but the context shifts. What was "cute" is now "calculated."
       What was "lazy" is now "exhausted." What was "cold" is now "protecting."
```

**Examples:**
- 病娇角色: Cut 1 sweet smile, carefully wrapping a gift, soft pink lighting → Cut 2 same smile, same angle, but the "gift" context is replaced by something unsettling (e.g., she's sitting in an empty room, surrounded by photos of one person)
- 隐退角色: Cut 1 clumsy waitress spilling coffee, comedy timing, warm light → Cut 2 same hands, now perfectly steady, drawing a complex magic circle in the dark (the "clumsiness" was an act)

**Key difference from High Contrast:** High Contrast says "she's BOTH of these things." Irony says "she was NEVER what you thought."

### 渐进揭示 / Progressive Reveal

**What it is:** Each cut peels back a layer, moving from macro to micro, from world to character to inner self. Classic "funnel" structure.

**When to use:**
- World Teasers (the default structure — wide → medium → close)
- Character introductions that need world context first
- Any video where the world itself is a selling point
- New worldview launches where audience has no prior context

**Design pattern (3-cut World Teaser):**
```
Cut 1: [world scale] — aerial/crane shot, the world in its grandest form
       The audience knows nothing yet. Give them the spectacle.
       Slow movement, epic scale, environmental atmosphere.

Cut 2: [character scale] — medium shots, introduce inhabitants
       Now populate the world. Show characters *in* their environments.
       Building momentum, faster pacing than Cut 1.

Cut 3: [conflict/intimacy scale] — close-up, the human moment
       The payoff. A relationship, a confrontation, an emotion.
       The audience now cares because they understand the world and the person.
```

**Design pattern (2-cut Character PV):**
```
Cut 1: [context] — character in their world, establishing who they *seem* to be
Cut 2: [revelation] — closer, more personal, the truth underneath
```

### 节奏对比 / Tempo Contrast

**What it is:** Adjacent cuts use dramatically different pacing — one slow and contemplative, the other fast and kinetic. The contrast in rhythm creates tension.

**When to use:**
- Action-oriented worldviews (martial arts, battle fantasy, sci-fi combat)
- Characters who alternate between stillness and explosive action
- Campaign goal = excitement / adrenaline
- World Teasers that want to build from calm to climax

**Design pattern:**
```
Cut 1 (slow): Static or ultra-slow camera movement. Character in peaceful moment.
              Long, lingering shot. Ambient sound emphasis.
              The audience relaxes into the stillness.

Cut 2 (fast): Sudden kinetic energy. Tracking shot, fast movement, particles, effects.
              The stillness was *before the storm*. Now the storm arrives.
              The contrast in rhythm is the emotional punch.
```

**Examples:**
- 修仙 PV: Cut 1 meditating on mountain peak, clouds drifting, 12s of near-stillness → Cut 2 sword flight combat, aerial tracking shot, spiritual energy explosions
- 赛博朋克: Cut 1 character staring at rain through neon-lit window, static camera → Cut 2 motorcycle chase through rain-slicked streets, handheld-feel camera

---

### 情绪升温 / Emotional Escalation

**What it is:** Each cut intensifies the *same* emotion rather than switching to a different one. The audience feels the emotion building.

**When to use:**
- Mood Pieces (though typically 1 cut, can extend to 2 for greater impact)
- Characters whose appeal is emotional depth rather than contrast
- 小红书 / emotional platform content where you want to make the audience *feel*
- Bittersweet, nostalgic, or melancholic characters

**Design pattern (2-cut extension):**
```
Cut 1: [seed emotion] — character in a moment that's slightly sad/warm/lonely
       Not yet at full intensity. A hint. A quiet version of the feeling.

Cut 2: [full bloom] — the same emotion, now overwhelming
       Same character, perhaps same setting, but something has shifted.
       The light changed. The expression deepened. The emptiness is now visible.
```

**Examples:**
- 治愈角色: Cut 1 character smiling while cooking alone, warm but slightly empty kitchen → Cut 2 same kitchen, now she's set two places but the second chair is empty, same warm light but the smile is different
- 遗忘角色: Cut 1 character walking through familiar hallway → Cut 2 same hallway, same walk, but everything is slightly faded/dusty — time has passed, she hasn't changed

---

### 视角跳转 / Perspective Shift

**What it is:** Adjacent cuts show the same moment or relationship from different viewpoints — character A's perspective vs character B's, or the character's view vs the world's view of them.

**When to use:**
- Multi-character campaigns (showing a relationship from both sides)
- Characters with unreliable self-perception
- Campaigns that want to highlight character dynamics
- "Who do you choose?" style engagement hooks

**Design pattern:**
```
Cut 1: [Perspective A] — how the character sees themselves or their situation
Cut 2: [Perspective B] — how the world (or another character) sees the same thing
       The difference between the two perspectives IS the story.
```

**Examples:**
- 关系动态: Cut 1 (her POV) watching him train from behind a pillar, gentle watching → Cut 2 (his POV) turning around, she's already pretending to read, but the book is upside down
- 自我认知差: Cut 1 character sees herself as ordinary (muted colors, small figure in frame) → Cut 2 the world sees her as extraordinary (saturated colors, heroic low-angle)

---

### 吸引力陷阱 / Attraction Trap

**What it is:** Cut 1 is designed to be so visually or emotionally *attractive* that it hooks the viewer, and Cut 2 deepens the hook by adding dimension. The goal is raw viewer retention in the first 3 seconds.

**When to use:**
- TikTok / short-form platforms where the first 3 seconds decide everything
- Characters with strong visual appeal or striking first impressions
- Acquisition campaigns where the audience has never seen this character
- "Scroll-stopper" content

**Design pattern:**
```
Cut 1: [the hook — maximum visual/emotional impact in 3 seconds]
       Most striking angle of the character. Unusual beauty, dramatic lighting,
       or an immediately intriguing action. The viewer stops scrolling.

Cut 2: [the depth — reward the viewer for staying]
       Now that they're watching, show something unexpected.
       The beautiful face has a scar. The elegant pose hides a weapon.
       The perfect smile is practiced in a mirror, alone.
```

## Strategy Selection Guide

Not every video needs a special strategy — the default **渐进揭示 / Progressive Reveal** (world → character → moment) works well for most content. Use this decision tree:

```
Q: Does this character have a hidden side or internal contradiction?
├── Yes → 高反差 (if both sides are genuine)
│         反讽 (if the public image is deliberately misleading)
├── No  → Q: Is the campaign goal emotional resonance?
│         ├── Yes → 情绪升温 (deepen one feeling)
│         │         吸引力陷阱 (hook → depth, for acquisition)
│         └── No  → Q: Is this a multi-character or world-focused video?
│                   ├── Yes → 渐进揭示 (world → character → conflict)
│                   │         视角跳转 (for relationship dynamics)
│                   └── No  → Q: Does the character oscillate between calm/action?
│                             ├── Yes → 节奏对比 (still → explosive)
│                             └── No  → 渐进揭示 (safe default)
```

**Combinations are valid.** A 3-cut World Teaser might use Progressive Reveal for Cuts 1→2 and High Contrast for Cuts 2→3. A Character PV might combine Attraction Trap (Cut 1) with Irony (Cut 2).

## Post-Production Reminders

Regardless of strategy:
- **All text (subtitles, titles, taglines) is added in post-production.** Never include text rendering in AI video prompts.
- **Generate 2-3 variants per cut**, select the best. AI video is stochastic.
- **Character consistency:** Generate a reference image from `concept-arts.md` first → use img2vid models (Wan 2.5, Seedance) with the reference image as starting point.
- **Transition editing:** The "transition" between cuts happens in editing software (CapCut / Premiere / DaVinci) — use hard cuts, dissolves, or whip pans depending on the strategy's energy level.
